Columns

Name: Name of the colleges
Type: Type of the colleges
1 = public
2 = private non-profit
3 = for-profit
Tier: Tier of the colleges
1 = Ivy Plus
2 = Other elite schools (public and private)
3 = Highly selective public
4 = Highly selective private
5 = Selective public
6 = Selective private
7 = Nonselective 4-year public
8 = Nonselective 4-year private not-for-profit
9 = Two-year (public and private not-for-profit)
10 = Four-year for-profit
11 = Two-year for-profit
12 = Less than two year schools of any type
13 = Attending college with insufficient data
14 = Not in college between the ages of 19-22
Tier Name: Name of college tier
1 = Four-year
2 = Two-year
3 = Less than Two-year
Count: Average number of kids per cohort
K_Median: Median child individual earnings in 2014 (rounded to the nearest $100)
Par_Median: Median parent household income (rounded to nearest $100)
Female: Fraction of females out of the entire cohort

Visualizations

Tableau Prototype

D3 Implementation

Size of bubbles indicate the average cohort size. The colors represent the range of the median of kids' earnings per college.
As the colleges are more competitive, the median of all the kids' earnings increased. This indicates that people are more likely to earn more money the more prestigious their school is. Class sizes fluctates greatly among different tier colleges.

Zillow Affordability Heat Map

The colors on the heat map represent the standardized value of female ratio. Orange values indicate a lower than normal female ratio. Blue values indicate higher than normal female ratio. Each rectangle on the heat map indicates the normalization of the median of parents' income, the college tier, and standardized female ratio.
Each collge tier generally has an equal normal distribution of parents' median income. Parents' median income was not substantially high or low based on the different college tiers. However, 2-year schools (not-for-profit) tended to have a lower than normal female ratio. On the other hand, selective public schools had a higher than average female ratio.
